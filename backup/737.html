Entries in Stationers' Register: July 27, 1639: Ordered that John Benson have leave to print an impression of 1500 of the tragedy of Albovine by Davenant, the copy never entered and therefore in the disposal of the Court: "Mr Benson desired leaue of the Cort. to print an Imprssion of the play called The Tragedy of Albouine made by Mr Davenant wch was printed in Anno 1629. & neuer entred & therefore in the disposall of this Cort. Vpon Consideracion thereof It was ordered that the said Mr Benson should haue leaue to print an Imprssion of 1500. paying to the Poore of this Company xls." (Court Book C, fol. 166b).
May 3, 1651: Transferred from John Marriot to Richard Marriot: "Salvo Iurie cujuscunque; (vizt:). ... Albovine the play."
Mar 7, 1653: Transferred from Richard Marriot to Humphrey Moseley: "a Copie or booke called The Tragedie of Albouine King of the Lombards. Written by Sr. Wm. Dauenant."
 
Additional Notes: There is no evidence that this play was ever acted, but Davenant may have written it with the intention that it would eventually be performed by a professional playing company.
