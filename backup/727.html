Variants: There are two issues of this edition. In 412b(i), the preliminaries contain verses in which the "Picture" is "plainly explained" [A1r] and an engraved title page [A2r]. In 412b(ii), the original half-sheet A was replaced by a cancel, which contains the "Prologue" [A1v] and the same explanatory verses [A2r]. See also Greg 412b(i)

Title-Page Features

Title: A GAME AT CHESSE. ... [in single column] A Game at Chesse is here displayde, | Betweene the Blacke and White-House made, | Wherein Crowne-thirsting Policy, | For the Blacke-House (by Falacy) | To the White-Knight, check, often giues, | And to some straites, him thereby driues; | The Fat-Blacke-Bishop help's also. | With faithlesse heart to giue the blow: | Yet (maugre all their craft) at length, | The White Knight, with wit-wondrous strength; | And circumspectiue Prudency, | Giues Check-mate by Discouery | To the Black Knight; and so at last | The game (thus) won, the Black-House cast | Into the Bagge, and therein shut, | Finde all their plumes and Cockes-combes cut. | Plaine-dealing (thus) by wisedomes guide, | Defeats the cheats of Craft and Pride.
Author:
Performance: As it was Acted nine Dayes together at the GLOBE on the Bank-side.
