Title-Page Features

Title: A new PLAY Called CANTERBURIE His Change of Diot. Which sheweth variety of wit and mirth:
Author:
Performance: privately acted neare the Palace-yard at Westminster. In the [in single column joined by pointed bracket] { 1 Act, the Bishop of Canterbury having variety of dainties, is not satisfied till he be fed with tippets of mens eares. | 2 Act, he hath his nose held to the Grinde-stone. | 3 Act, he is put into a bird Cage with the Confessor. | 4 Act, The Jester tells the King the Story.
Illustration: Woodcut on title page of Laud and his confessor in a bird cage, mocked by a fool
Imprint: Printed Anno Domini, 1641.
