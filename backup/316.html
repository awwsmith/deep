Total Editions: 2 quartos
 
Variants: There are two issues of this edition: Greg 177b(i) contains the original title leaf dated 1616, with the statement that play is "Newly corrected" and the imprint listing Stansby as printer and Knight as publisher; Greg 177b(ii) contains a cancel title leaf dated 1618, which is printed in black and red, adds a woodcut image, replaces "Newly corrected" with "The Actors 12. men, and 4. women," and lists Fosbrooke as publisher. See also Greg 177b(i)

Title-Page Features

Title: IACKE DRVMS Entertainment. OR THE COMEDIE OF PASQVIL AND KATHERINE.
Author:
Performance: As it hath beene sundry times Playd, by the Children of Powles. The Actors 12. men, and 4. women.
Illustration: Woodcut of an extravagantly dressed man on title page
Imprint: LONDON Printed for Nathanaell Fosbrooke. 1618.

Paratextual Material

Character List: "The names of all the Men and Women, that act this Play" [I4v]

Stationer Information

Printer: Stansby, William
Publisher: Fosbrooke, Nathaniel
Entries in Stationers' Register: Sep 8, 1600: Entered to Felix Norton: "a booke Called Iohn Drums enterteynment A commedy as yt hathe ben diuerse tymes Acted by the Children of Paules".
Oct 23, 1600: Transferred from Felix Norton to Richard Oliffe: "A booke called Iohn Dromes enterteinement as yt hathe ben Acted by the Children of paules".
Nov 6, 1615: Transferred from the widow of Richard Oliffe to Philip Knight: "Iacke drumes Intertaynement".
 
Additional Notes: Title page in red and black.
