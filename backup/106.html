Title-Page Features

Title: A DISCOVRSE OF The Queenes Maiesties entertainement in Suffolk and Norffolk: With a description of many things then presently seene. ... with diuers shewes of his own inuention sette out at Norwich: and some rehearsal of hir Highnesse retourne from Progresse. Wherevnto is adioyned a commendation of Sir Humfrey Gilberts ventrous iourney.
Author: Deuised by THOMAS CHVRCHYARDE, Gent.
Performance:
Imprint: AT LONDON, Imprinted by Henrie Bynneman, seruante to the right Honourable Sir CHRISTOFER HATTON Vizchamberlayne.

Dedication: "The Epistle Dedicatorie" to: Gilbert Gerard (judge); from: Thomas Churchyard [A2r]
To the Reader: "To the Reader" [B1r]
Explicit: FINIS q Tho. Churchyard. [H1r]
Other Paratexts: List of gentlemen knighted [H1v]; "A matter touching the Iourney of Sir Humfrey Gilbarte Knight" (in verse) [H2r]; "A welcome home to Master Martin Frobusher" (in verse) signed "Thomas Churchyarde" [L1r]

Stationer Information

Printer: Bynneman, Henry
Entries in Stationers' Register: Sep 20, 1578: Entered to Henry Bynneman: "the enterteignemt of the .Q. Matie. in Suffolk and Norffolk. gathered by Tho. Churchyard."
 
Additional Notes: Descriptions printed in black letter, speeches in roman and italic. Dedicatory epistle exists in three states and is variously addressed to "Gilbert Gerard," "Gilbert Gerrard," and "William Iarret" (an error for Gilbert Gerard).
