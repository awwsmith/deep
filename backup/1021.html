Title-Page Features

Title: DING DONG, OR Sr. Pitifull Parliament, On his Death-Bed. His Pulses felt by Doctor KING, and his water cast by Doctor BISHOP. His last Will, and Testament, with his Death, Buriall, and Epitaph. ... [in single column] Hast, hast, good Sexton, toule the bell; | Even at the point of death | Lies our most blessed Parliament, | And scarce can draw his breath. | Goe call the Doctors; Priviledge, | Thou art his serving creature. | Tell Doctor King, he needs must come, | To helpe restore his Nature. | Runne Directory, hast I say, | Call Doctor Bishop hither; | Tell him, our dying Parliament, | Want him, and King together. | O Nol, O Tom, O Rainsborow, | O Devill, Foole, and Knave. | Come close the eyes of your Deare State, | And lay him in the grave.
Author: By Mercurius Melancholicus.
